Kaliyuga is the age in which we dwell and is the least auspicious period of the four yugas. There are specifically 432,000 years in Kaliyuga. Kaliyuga is defined by religious and moral deterioration. Humans worship false god\/s, and people practice religious rites for worldly pleasures. They don’t practice their spiritual duties. Natural calamities like starvation and pestilence often occur\/s. Bad people become more powerful than good ones.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The story goes that after a great sacrifice at the end of the Dwapara Yuga ( Krishna’s last appearance on earth), Vishnu and Brahma took a nap. A small insect woke up Vishnu and asked him to take care of him when he reawakened. When Vishnu awoke, he had forgotten about his promise, and so the insect appeared in front of him as Kali, who was a demon with an insatiable thirst for blood.<\/p>\n\n\n

In the Kaliyuga, everything is hued in the following range: Black skin, black hair, a body odor resembling that of a donkey, and people who don’t mind the scent of their clothes and homes. The primary food is bread, and clothing is made of cotton and deer. The river water is used to make the water for drinking. Kali Yuga residents have a limited lifespan( about 100 years only). <\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

The ultimate age of creation, the Kaliyuga, sees a steady decline in human values. Human minds are brittle and heavily influenced by the senses. People frequently act unreasonably. Most people act impulsively because the intellect is brittle and sensations are intense. The material world’s influence has weakened the mind while strengthening the senses.<\/p>\n
